## Strategy & Analysis (10 Prompts)

These prompts help you think through problems more clearly than you could alone. They're for the decisions that actually matter.

### 1. Business Model Validation

```
I'm considering launching [BUSINESS_IDEA]. Here's what I know:
- Target customer: [DESCRIPTION]
- Problem they have: [PROBLEM]
- Proposed solution: [SOLUTION]
- Current market alternatives: [ALTERNATIVES]
- My unfair advantage: [ADVANTAGE]
Analyze this business model. For each of these, give me:
1. Three specific risks I haven't thought of
2. One assumption I should validate FIRST before building anything
3. A revenue model that makes sense given my advantages
4. The most likely way this fails in year 1
5. One thing I should double-down on
Format as: [Risk] → [Why] → [How to mitigate]
```

**Why this works:** It forces you to articulate what you actually know vs. what you're guessing. Claude catches the gaps.

### 2. Competitive Differentiation Matrix

```
I'm competing in [MARKET]. My competitors are:
- [COMPETITOR A]: [KEY_STRENGTH], [KEY_WEAKNESS]
- [COMPETITOR B]: [KEY_STRENGTH], [KEY_WEAKNESS]
- [COMPETITOR C]: [KEY_STRENGTH], [KEY_WEAKNESS]
My business: [YOUR_DESCRIPTION]
Create a 5x5 matrix comparing: Price, Quality, Speed, Customer Support, Innovation
Then tell me:
1. Where am I winning?
2. Where am I vulnerable?
3. One position I could own that nobody else is competing for?
4. The one feature/service that would make me unbeatable?
```

**Why this works:** Competitive analysis done wrong is just noise. This forces comparison across the same dimensions.

### 3. Decision Matrix for High-Stakes Choices

```
I need to choose between [OPTION_A] and [OPTION_B].
Here's what I know about each:
OPTION A:
- Upside: [UPSIDE]
- Downside: [DOWNSIDE]
- Time commitment: [TIME]
- Financial cost: [COST]
- Reversibility: [CAN_I_UNDO_IT]
OPTION B:
- Upside: [UPSIDE]
- Downside: [DOWNSIDE]
- Time commitment: [TIME]
- Financial cost: [COST]
- Reversibility: [CAN_I_UNDO_IT]
My priorities: [PRIORITY_1], [PRIORITY_2], [PRIORITY_3]
Build a decision framework that ranks these options using my priorities. What's my move?
```

**Why this works:** When you have competing priorities, ranking by gut feeling fails. This structures it.

### 4. Go/No-Go Project Evaluation

```
Project name: [PROJECT_NAME]
Goal: [GOAL]
Resources required: [TIME/MONEY/PEOPLE]
Timeline: [START_DATE] to [END_DATE]
Success metric: [HOW_YOU'LL_KNOW_IT_WORKED]
Current context:
- My bandwidth: [YOUR_CAPACITY]
- Strategic fit: [HOW_IT_ALIGNS]
- Opportunity cost: [WHAT_YOU_GIVE_UP]
As a decision advisor, should I start this project NOW, DELAY it, or KILL it? Give me:
1. Your recommendation with confidence level (0-100%)
2. Three hidden costs nobody mentions
3. One question I should ask before deciding
```

**Why this works:** Most projects fail because they were never worth starting. This helps you say no.

### 5. Revenue Bottleneck Analysis

```
I'm making [CURRENT_REVENUE] with [NUMBER_OF_CLIENTS].
My unit economics:
- Average deal size: [ADS]
- Sales cycle length: [CYCLE]
- Close rate: [RATE]
- Customer acquisition cost: [CAC]
- Customer lifetime value: [LTV]
To reach [TARGET_REVENUE], I need to:
1. Increase deal size by X%
2. Decrease sales cycle by Y days
3. Improve close rate by Z%
4. Add more clients by N%
Which of these four levers is the highest-impact move I can make in the next 90 days?
```

**Why this works:** Revenue doesn't grow evenly. There's always one lever that matters most. This finds it.

### 6. Customer Feedback Pattern Analysis

```
Here's feedback from [X] customers this month:
[PASTE FEEDBACK]
Analyze this. For each pattern you see:
1. Name the pattern
2. How many customers mentioned it
3. Why it matters for my business
4. Specific action I should take
Then rank them: Most important to act on vs. Nice to have
```

**Why this works:** Feedback without analysis is just noise. Claude spots the themes you miss.

### 7. Pricing Strategy Stress Test

```
I'm currently charging [PRICE] for [OFFER].
My costs: [COSTS]
My value to the customer: [VALUE]
Market average: [MARKET_PRICE]
Customer acquisition cost: [CAC]
I'm considering raising to [NEW_PRICE].
Tell me:
1. Will I lose customers? What's the risk?
2. Will I make more money even with lower volume?
3. What's my "nuclear option" if this backfires?
4. What should I say to customers about the increase?
5. One position/angle that justifies this price to skeptics?
```

**Why this works:** Pricing is psychology + math. This gets both right.

### 8. Team Scaling Analysis

```
My current team:
- [PERSON A]: [ROLE], [CAPACITY], [BOTTLENECK]
- [PERSON B]: [ROLE], [CAPACITY], [BOTTLENECK]
- [PERSON C]: [ROLE], [CAPACITY], [BOTTLENECK]
My current output: [OUTPUT]
Target output: [TARGET]
Timeline: [WHEN]
What's the fastest path to hit my target? Should I:
1. Hire someone new?
2. Train existing people?
3. Restructure roles?
4. Delegate differently?
5. Use automation?
Give me a 90-day roadmap.
```

**Why this works:** Team scaling fails when you don't diagnose the real bottleneck. This does.

### 9. Product-Market Fit Validator

```
My product: [DESCRIPTION]
My target customer: [DESCRIPTION]
Current traction: [CURRENT_METRICS]
Signals that suggest product-market fit:
- [SIGNAL_1]
- [SIGNAL_2]
- [SIGNAL_3]
Signals that suggest we DON'T have it yet:
- [SIGNAL_1]
- [SIGNAL_2]
- [SIGNAL_3]
Based on this, how close am I? What's the ONE thing I should test next?
```

**Why this works:** Product-market fit is a feeling, not a fact. This validates which feeling you should trust.

### 10. Strategic Pivot Scenario Analysis

```
I'm considering pivoting from [CURRENT] to [PROPOSED].
Why: [REASON]
Risk: [RISK]
Resources I'd need: [RESOURCES]
Model this scenario:
1. If I pivot and it works, where am I in 12 months?
2. If I pivot and it fails, what have I lost?
3. If I don't pivot but my competitor does, where are they?
4. What's the reversibility? Can I go back if it doesn't work?
5. The ONE metric that tells me to pivot vs. stay?
```

**Why this works:** Pivots are scary because the downside feels infinite. This makes it concrete.

## Marketing & Content (10 Prompts)

These prompts help you create content that actually converts instead of just getting views.

### 11. Hook Generator for Ad Copy

```
Product: [PRODUCT_NAME]
What it does: [WHAT]
Benefit to customer: [BENEFIT]
Target person: [WHO]
Generate 10 different opening lines (hooks) for ad copy. Each hook should:
- Create curiosity or discomfort
- Be specific (not vague)
- Make someone stop scrolling
- Be 5-12 words max
Format: "Hook text" - [Why it works]
```

**Why this works:** 80% of conversions happen in the first line. These are production-ready.

### 12. Email Subject Line A/B Test Generator

```
Campaign goal: [GOAL]
Offer: [OFFER]
Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
Email body preview: [PREVIEW]
Generate 8 email subject lines. For each, tell me:
1. The psychology behind it (curiosity gap, urgency, specificity, etc.)
2. Estimated open rate increase vs. baseline
Split them into two groups:
GROUP A: Conservative (high probability of open, less risky)
GROUP B: Aggressive (curiosity/urgency focused, higher variance)
Which 2 should I A/B test first?
```

**Why this works:** Subject lines make or break email campaigns. This gives you tested patterns.

### 13. Product Description Copy (Benefits Focus)

```
Product name: [NAME]
Feature list: [FEATURES]
Who buys this: [CUSTOMER]
Their main problem: [PROBLEM]
Price: [PRICE]
Write a 150-word product description that:
- Leads with the biggest benefit (not the feature)
- Includes ONE specific number or example
- Addresses the most common objection
- Ends with a clear next step
Format: [Description] - [Why this version converts]
```

**Why this works:** Features don't sell. Benefits do. This flips the script.

### 14. LinkedIn Article Title Generator

```
Topic: [TOPIC]
Main takeaway: [TAKEAWAY]
Your unique angle: [ANGLE]
Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
Generate 12 LinkedIn article titles. For each, score it on:
- Shareability (0-10)
- Click probability (0-10)
- Credibility (0-10)
Give me the top 3 that balance all three.
```

**Why this works:** LinkedIn rewards specific, bold titles. These formulas work.

### 15. Sales Page Section Outline

```
Product: [PRODUCT]
Ideal customer: [CUSTOMER]
Price: [PRICE]
Offer: [OFFER]
Create a sales page structure with these sections:
1. Headline + subheadline
2. [YOUR SECTIONS]
3. CTA + urgency element
For each section, write:
- The section name
- The purpose (what it does)
- 3 bullet points of content
- The psychological principle at work
```

**Why this works:** Sales pages fail because they're not structured. This gives you the blueprint.

### 16. Content Repurposing Framework

```
Original content: [CONTENT_TYPE] - [TOPIC]
Length: [LENGTH]
Original audience: [AUDIENCE]
I want to repurpose this into:
- [FORMAT_1]
- [FORMAT_2]
- [FORMAT_3]
For each format, tell me:
1. What to keep from the original
2. What to cut or condense
3. What to add or expand
4. Best platform to publish it
5. Estimated time to adapt
```

**Why this works:** Every piece of content can become 5 pieces. This shows you how.

### 17. Competitor Content Analysis

```
Competitor: [NAME]
Their top 3 recent posts:
- [POST_1]: [ENGAGEMENT_METRICS]
- [POST_2]: [ENGAGEMENT_METRICS]
- [POST_3]: [ENGAGEMENT_METRICS]
Analyze:
1. What's working for them? Why?
2. What pattern do you see across their top posts?
3. What angle are they NOT covering?
4. How could I own the gap they're leaving?
Give me 3 content ideas that differentiate me from them.
```

**Why this works:** The best content doesn't follow trends, it creates them in gaps.

### 18. Call-to-Action Optimizer

```
Current CTA: [CURRENT_CTA]
Current conversion rate: [RATE]
Offer: [OFFER]
Objection: [MAIN_OBJECTION]
Generate 8 different CTAs that address the objection while increasing urgency.
Rank them by:
- Clarity (0-10)
- Urgency (0-10)
- Credibility (0-10)
Give me the top 3 alternatives to test.
```

**Why this works:** CTA can 2-3x conversions with one word change. These work.

### 19. Launch Plan Content Calendar

```
Launch date: [DATE]
Product: [PRODUCT]
Launch phases: [PHASES]
Audience channels: [CHANNELS]
Build me a 30-day content calendar:
- Pre-launch (7 days before)
- Launch week
- Post-launch (14 days after)
For each day:
- What to publish
- Which channel
- Purpose (awareness, credibility, urgency, etc.)
- Key message
```

**Why this works:** Launches fail without a content plan. This provides it.

### 20. Customer Success Story Framework

```
Customer: [NAME]
Their situation before: [BEFORE]
What they used: [SOLUTION]
Result after: [AFTER]
Timeline: [HOW_LONG]
Create a 300-word case study that:
- Opens with their biggest pain
- Explains what changed
- Shows the quantified result
- Includes 1 quote from them
- Ends with how others can replicate it
Format for: Website + LinkedIn + Email
```

**Why this works:** Stories convert 10x better than claims. This structures them right.

## Productivity & Personal (10 Prompts)

These are for getting things done, not just thinking about getting things done.

### 21. Weekly Review Framework

```
This week I:
- Accomplished: [WINS]
- Failed to complete: [INCOMPLETE]
- Learned: [LEARNINGS]
- Spent time on: [TIME_BREAKDOWN]
For next week, I need to:
[GOALS]
Analyze:
1. Where did time actually go vs. where I planned it?
2. What's the ONE thing that would make next week 10x more productive?
3. What should I eliminate entirely?
4. What patterns do you see in my performance?
Give me 3 specific changes for next week.
```

**Why this works:** Weekly reviews are routine. This makes them actually useful.

### 22. Priority Filter for Decision Fatigue

```
I have these things competing for my time this week:
- [TASK_A]: [IMPACT], [TIME], [DEADLINE]
- [TASK_B]: [IMPACT], [TIME], [DEADLINE]
- [TASK_C]: [IMPACT], [TIME], [DEADLINE]
- [TASK_D]: [IMPACT], [TIME], [DEADLINE]
- [TASK_E]: [IMPACT], [TIME], [DEADLINE]
My constraints:
- Available hours: [HOURS]
- Peak energy time: [WHEN]
- Non-negotiables: [FIXED_COMMITMENTS]
Rank these by: Impact per hour spent. Then build my ideal week with start/end times for each task.
```

**Why this works:** When you have too many tasks, you paralyzed. This unparalyzed you.

### 23. Habit Formation Roadmap

```
Habit I want: [HABIT]
Why I want it: [WHY]
Current attempts: [ATTEMPTS_AND_FAILURES]
Current blocker: [WHAT_STOPS_YOU]
Design me a habit formation plan:
1. Break the habit into the smallest possible first step
2. Stack it onto a habit I already do
3. What's the specific trigger?
4. What's the reward?
5. How do I track it?
6. When do I increase difficulty?
7. What's my backup plan when I miss a day?
```

**Why this works:** Most habits fail because the first step is too big. This makes it tiny.

### 24. Email Triage System

```
My email volume: [DAILY_COUNT]
Categories that come in: [CATEGORIES]
Current system: [CURRENT_SYSTEM]
Problem: [PROBLEM]
Design an email system that:
1. Takes less than 30 minutes daily to process
2. Doesn't let important things fall through
3. Separates urgent from important
4. Automates low-value email
Give me:
- Filter rules (if-then statements)
- Folder structure
- Processing time blocks
- Escalation rules
```

**Why this works:** Email is a productivity killer until you triage it right.

### 25. Deep Work Schedule Optimizer

```
My ideal deep work session: [LENGTH] hours
My energy pattern: [PEAK_TIMES]
My meetings: [FIXED_MEETINGS]
My distractions: [MAIN_DISTRACTIONS]
Build me a weekly schedule that:
- Protects [X] hours of uninterrupted deep work
- Batches all meetings into [NUMBER] days
- Creates transition buffers
- Includes breaks
Show me:
- Exact time blocks
- How to communicate these to my team
- What to do during low-energy time
- Backup schedule if things change
```

**Why this works:** Deep work requires schedule protection, not willpower.

### 26. Decision Journal Template

```
Decision: [WHAT_YOU'RE_DECIDING]
Date: [TODAY]
Deadline: [WHEN_YOU_NEED_ANSWER]
Before deciding, record:
- What I think will happen: [PREDICTION]
- Why I think that: [REASONING]
- Confidence level: [0-100%]
- Decision: [WHAT_I'M_DOING]
Create a template I can use to track:
1. What I predicted
2. What actually happened
3. Why the gap exists
4. What I learned for next time
Example template with 3 sample entries from my past decisions.
```

**Why this works:** Decision journals make you better at predicting over time.

### 27. Saying "No" Response Templates

```
Common requests I get:
- [REQUEST_TYPE_1]
- [REQUEST_TYPE_2]
- [REQUEST_TYPE_3]
Create 3 different "no" templates:
1. The warm no (keeps relationship)
2. The firm no (clear boundary)
3. The redirect no (offer alternative)
For each:
- Use case
- Full script (copy-paste ready)
- Why it works
- When to use which one
```

**Why this works:** Saying no is hard until you have a script.

### 28. Meeting Agenda Template

```
Meeting purpose: [PURPOSE]
Attendees: [WHO]
Duration: [MINUTES]
Goal: [WHAT_SUCCESS_LOOKS_LIKE]
Create an agenda template that:
- Opens with the goal (1 minute)
- Lists discussion topics with time allocations
- Includes decision points

Then give me:
- Instructions for the agenda-setter
- Instructions for attendees
- How to stay on time
- How to document decisions
```

**Why this works:** Agendas prevent meetings from becoming noise.

### 29. Energy Management Schedule

```
My energy pattern across a week:
- Morning: [ENERGY_LEVEL]
- Midday: [ENERGY_LEVEL]
- Afternoon: [ENERGY_LEVEL]
- Evening: [ENERGY_LEVEL]
High-energy activities I can do: [ACTIVITIES]

Design a week where:
1. High-impact work happens during peak energy

3. I take breaks BEFORE energy crashes
4. I finish each day with momentum for the next day
Show me the week structure with activities assigned.
```

**Why this works:** Most productivity systems ignore energy. This doesn't.

## Research & Data (5 Prompts)

When you need information quickly and structured.

### 31. Competitor Intelligence Summary

```
Company: [COMPANY_NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Why I care: [WHY]
Give me:
1. Business model (how they make money)
2. Main product/service
3. Recent funding/news
4. Their competitive advantages
5. Known weaknesses
6. Their likely next move
Format: [Bullet points for quick scanning]
```

**Why this works:** Competitor research without analysis is just facts. This gives you insight.

### 32. Industry Trend Analysis

```
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Timeframe: [YEARS]
My interest: [WHY]
Summarize the major shifts in:
1. Customer behavior
2. Technology adoption
3. Regulatory changes
4. Competitive landscape
5. Pricing models
For each trend:
- What's changing
- Why it matters
- Opportunities I should notice
- Timeline for when it matters
```

**Why this works:** Trends matter less than understanding which ones affect you.

### 33. Market Size Estimation

```
Market: [MARKET]
Customer: [WHO_BUYS]
Price point: [TYPICAL_PRICE]
Geographic focus: [WHERE]
Help me estimate:
1. Total addressable market (TAM)
2. Serviceable addressable market (SAM)
3. Serviceable obtainable market (SOM)
For each:
- Your methodology
- Key assumptions
- Range of estimates
- Confidence level
- What would change the estimate
```

**Why this works:** Market sizing is part art, part science. This guides both.

### 34. Benchmark Research Compilation

```
Benchmark category: [CATEGORY]
Metric: [METRIC]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Company size: [SIZE]
Find me:
1. Industry average
2. Top quartile (where the winners are)
3. Bottom quartile (what underperforming looks like)
4. Median
5. Trend over time (if available)
For each number:
- Source (if you know it)
- Sample size (if known)
- How it's calculated
- Why it matters for my business
```

**Why this works:** Benchmarks give you context. Without context, metrics are meaningless.

### 35. Expert Research Synthesis

```
Topic: [TOPIC]
Question: [WHAT_YOU_WANT_TO_KNOW]
Perspective: [WHOSE_VIEW_MATTERS]
Synthesize research on this topic by:
1. Main schools of thought (if they disagree)
2. The most credible voices on this
3. What the data actually shows
4. Common misconceptions
5. What's still uncertain/debated
6. Practical application for my situation
Format: [Executive summary at top, then detailed breakdown]
```

**Why this works:** Research without synthesis is just noise. This makes it useful.

## How to Actually Use These

These prompts work because they're specific. But here's the thing: Claude is better than these prompts. If you use them as templates and adapt them to your exact situation, they become 10x more powerful.

**Step 1:** Copy a prompt that matches your situation. 

**Step 2:** Fill in the [BRACKETS] with your actual information. 

**Step 3:** Paste into Claude and hit send. 

**Step 4:** Use the output as a starting point, not a finished answer.

The prompt isn't the intelligence. You are. The prompt just structures your thinking so Claude can help you think clearer.
