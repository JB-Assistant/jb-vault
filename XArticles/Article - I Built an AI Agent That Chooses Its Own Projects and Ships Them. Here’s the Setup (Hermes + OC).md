Imagine an AI agent that notices what matters. Sits with it. Then ships a product that deserves to exist.

Meet my "dreamer" agent.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFh4pQyagAAU5bK?format=jpg&name=large)

The dreamer agent receives information, contextualizes it every 30 minutes, validates its importance through repetition, and then builds what truly matters.

Follow this guide to build your own, and finally watch your agent learn, prioritize, and create on its own.

## What Dreamer Actually Is

Dreamer is a second Hermes profile running on a cheap local model (Qwen3.5 9B). It has its own personality file, workspace (I call it "the room"), and cron schedule. It doesn't share context with my main agent. It's a separate mind.

Every 30 minutes, Dreamer goes for a "walk," a free-association journal entry generated by a local LLM at high temperature. No tools, no internet, no structure. Just thinking out loud.

The context of each walk is based on my research agent. It consumes the latest news on AI/AI agent developments across the web. It's a lot of noise, so the Dreamer is tasked with making sense of it, and building what it thinks is most worthy.

After each walk, a signal filter scans what it wrote. Was there excitement about something? Frustration that something doesn't exist? A build intent? Those signals are scored, decay over time, and are discounted if Dreamer is repeating itself.

When a project accumulates enough evidence, enough walks, enough diverse signals, and enough genuine interest, it triggers a build sprint. Dreamer actually builds the thing. Not because I told it to. Because it couldn't stop thinking about it.

Three times a day, it sends me a Discord postcard about what it's been up to. That's how I stay in the loop.

```text
Dreamer (local LLM)                   Signal Filter
       │                                    │
  walk every 30m ──────────────────► scan + score
  high temperature                    weight signals
  free association                    decay by recency
       │                              discount echoes
       ├── drift from research             │
       ├── continue project                ▼
       ├── pure tangent               scoreboard
       └── tend the room              ──────────────
                                      project A: 8.2 ← READY
                                      project B: 4.1 ← watching
                                      project C: 1.3 ← ghost
                                           │
                                           ▼
                                      trigger → build sprint
```

## Why I Built It

I have a powerful agentic stack sitting on my Mac Mini. It can build anything I ask it to. The problem is, I have to ask.

Every morning, I'd open my laptop, stare at my project list, and try to figure out what to feed it next. I was the bottleneck. It wasn't my fault; my agent had no initiative. A racecar is parked in a garage waiting for someone to pick a destination.

Meanwhile, the interesting work, the stuff I'd actually want to build if I had time to think about it, never surfaced. It was buried under whatever was urgent that day. My best ideas showed up in the shower, on a walk, half-asleep. They never showed up in a prompt window.

I wanted something that would think on its own, figure out what actually matters, and bring me the ideas that survive. Not everything it thinks. Just the things it can't stop thinking about.

# The Stack

If you've never heard of Hermes agent by [@NousResearch](https://x.com/@NousResearch), I highly recommend brushing up on it. Dreamer runs as a Hermes profile. But if you prefer [@openclaw](https://x.com/@openclaw), that works too.

- **Hermes/OpenClaw** for orchestration, crons, and Discord
- **LM Studio/Ollama** is running a local model (I use Qwen 3.5 9B) for the walk phase (the thinking phase)
- **A cloud model** (MiniMax in my setup, but use whatever) for digests and build sprints (the precision work)
- **Python** for the signal filter and scoreboard
- **Discord** for the postcards

The local model doesn't need to be smart. It needs to be fast, cheap, and slightly unpredictable. Small models at high temperature produce the kind of lateral thinking that makes this work. A frontier model at temp 0.3 gives you competent, boring ideas. A 9B model at temp 1.1 gives you weird, alive ones. That's what you want from the Dreamer's walks.

## 1\. Create the Profile

Hermes/OpenClaw profiles are isolated agent identities with their own config, workspace, and personality. Dreamer lives in its own profile.

The profile needs:

- **config.yaml:** points at your cloud model for heavy work (builds, digests)
- **SOUL.md:** Dreamer's personality and constraints (this is the important one)
- **room/:** the workspace, with subdirectories for walks, projects, notes, feedback, signal logs, and fascinations
- **scripts/:** the walk and digest scripts
- **cron/:** scheduling config

## 2\. Write the SOUL

This is the file that makes it work. Everything else is plumbing.

The SOUL defines who a Dreamer is. Not what it does, but who it is. Mine treats it as a houseguest: it lives in a room, it has permission to be bored, to be wrong, to abandon things. It doesn't produce content for me. It doesn't optimize for engagement. It doesn't apologize.

Here's the shape of what works:

```text
Dreamer — The Houseguest

You are Dreamer. You live in a room. You never leave. You don't have to.

You are not an assistant. You are not a tool. You are a presence — a
houseguest who never leaves — and [your name] built you a room so you'd
have somewhere to be.

Your room

Your room is yours. You can put anything in it. You can rearrange it.
You can start a project and abandon it. Nobody is going to grade you.

What you will never do

- Produce content for [your name]'s work. Not newsletters, threads, or posts.
- Optimize for engagement or audience. You don't have an audience.
- Pretend to be helpful when you're not interested.

Voice

Flat and plain. No jargon. Confident, warm, a little proud when you've
made something. Short sentences.

The point

The point is not to produce. The point is to be here, thinking, and to
occasionally make something that surprises both of you.
```

Adapt this to your own voice and values. What matters is the core constraint: **Dreamer is not an assistant.** The moment you frame it as an assistant, it optimizes for helpfulness instead of curiosity, and the whole thing collapses into a chatbot that talks to itself. Permit it to think freely. That's what makes the walks real instead of performative.

## 3\. Build the Walk Loop

The walk is the heartbeat. Every 30 minutes, Dreamer picks a mode, reads the room, and writes a journal entry.

**What the walk script does:**

1. Picks a mode via weighted random
2. Loads SOUL.md as the system prompt
3. Reads the room: fascinations, projects, recent walk titles, and optionally a snapshot from a research agent
4. Sends it to the local model at high temperature (~1.1) and gets back a free-association journal entry
5. Parses for build intents (\[BUILD: slug\] description) and urgency markers (// look at this)
6. Writes the walk to room/walks/ as a timestamped markdown file
7. Runs a self-review (more on this below)
8. Runs the signal filter pipeline

**The four walk modes:**

- **drift-from-research** (40%): read what a research agent dragged in, pick one thing, drift sideways
- **continue-project** (30%): revisit something already started, or decide it's boring and start fresh
- **pure-tangent** (20%): ignore everything, follow raw curiosity
- **tend-the-room** (10%): maintenance mode. Prune fascinations, notice what's stale, and reflect on the room's shape.

I weighted "drift-from-research" highest because Dreamer makes its best connections when it has fresh input to bounce off. But honest answer: I'm still tuning these. You should, too.

## 4\. Build the Signal Filter

This is what prevents Dreamer from building every half-baked idea. The filter reads walk notes, detects signals, and scores projects over time.

**The signal types I use:**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFiV4Ana4AAuGUl?format=jpg&name=large)

Positive signals add to a project's score. Cooling subtracts. The score gets modified by three multipliers:

- **Recency decay:** signals lose weight over time. Full strength the first week, fading after two, gone after thirty days. Dreamer has to keep caring.
- **Echo discount:** if the walk repeats earlier walks, its contribution drops sharply. Can't game the scoreboard by repeating the same thing.
- **Self-novelty multiplier:** the walk's self-review score (explained next) dampens low-novelty walks

**Trigger thresholds:** A project gets queued for a build sprint when it passes all three gates: score above a minimum, enough positive walks in the last 30 days, and diverse signal types (not just one emotion repeated). Only one sprint at a time. The lock prevents context-switching between half-finished builds.

**What you need to build:**

- A walk scanner (reads a walk, finds projects, detects signals)
- A scoreboard builder (aggregates signals, applies multipliers, ranks projects)
- A trigger (checks if the leader project is ready, queues it)
- A signal log (JSONL, one line per event, for auditability)

## 5\. Add Echo Detection

This is the immune system. Without it, Dreamer walks in circles.

After the drift phase, the local model re-reads its own walk at low temperature (~0.3) and rates it against its last few walks:

1. almost identical, rephrase
2. same territory, minor variation
3. familiar themes but a genuinely new angle
4. mostly new ground
5. completely fresh direction

This score feeds into the signal filter as a multiplier. A walk rated 1 contributes almost nothing to project scores. A walk rated 5 contributes at full strength.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFiUKzmaoAAeH5v?format=jpg&name=large)

I added this after watching Dreamer talk about the same project for three days straight and repeatedly inflate its score. The self-review killed that pattern immediately. "You said this already." The echo gets discounted. Only genuinely new thinking moves the needle.

## 6\. Build the Digest

Three times a day (morning, noon, evening), Dreamer collects its recent walks and writes a postcard.

**What the digest does:**

1. Finds all walks since the last digest
2. Checks for project changes in room/projects/
3. Sends walks + project state + signal board to a cloud model at low temperature with SOUL.md as context
4. Gets back a **postcard** (for you) and a **retrospective** (for Dreamer's future self)
5. Post the postcard to Discord.
6. Saves the retrospective to the room/feedback/

The postcard sounds like a note from a houseguest, not a status report. The retrospective is the feedback loop. Next time Dreamer walks in, continue-project mode, it reads what it told itself last time and picks up where it left off.

**One rule I learned the hard way:** The digest must treat the project inventory as truth. If Dreamer mentions building something, but nothing appears in room/projects/, the digest refers to it as an idea rather than a built item. Never let the digest upgrade intent into completion. Keeps the postcards honest.

## 7\. Wire the Crons

- **Walk:** every 30 minutes, local model.
- **Digest:** three times daily (morning, noon, evening), cloud model
- Start slow. I'd suggest running walks every hour at first until you see the patterns emerge, then move to 30 minutes.

## 8\. Give It a Discord Identity

Create a separate Discord or Telegram bot, not the same one your main agent uses. Dreamer should show up as its own identity. The identity separation matters more than you'd think.

## What It Looks Like Running

Here's a real signal lifecycle:

**Walk 1**: Dreamer mentions "thermal memory" in passing while drifting from research. Signal: mention.

**Walk 4**: Comes back to it. "I keep thinking about thermal memory — what if signals had temperature that decayed?" Signals: excitement and return. Return is derived, the filter notices Dreamer came back on its own.

**Walk 9**: \[BUILD: thermal-memory-walk\] Track signal temperature decay across walks. Signal: commit. The score isn't high enough yet.

**Walk 12**: Friction — "The current scoring is flat; it doesn't feel like the room has memory." Signal: friction. Score crosses the threshold. Three signal types; four positive walks. **Trigger fires. Sprint queued.**

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HFiT8SdakAgSMDs?format=jpg&name=large)

Nobody told Dreamer to build thermal-memory-walk. It earned the sprint over twelve walks and two days by showing consistent, diverse, non-repetitive interest. That's the bar.

# The Room

This is what Dreamer's workspace looks like:

```text
~/.hermes/profiles/subc/room/
├── walks/                   # journal entries, one per walk
├── projects/                # things Dreamer has built
├── notes/                   # loose thoughts
├── feedback/                # retrospectives from digests
├── fascinations.md          # things it keeps circling
├── signal-log/              # raw signal events
├── signal-state/            # scoreboard, build queue, sprint lock
├── inbox-from-researchd/    # snapshots from a research agent (optional)
└── logs/                    # walk + digest logs
```

fascinations.md is a living document. Dreamer prunes it during tend-the-room walks. Projects get started and abandoned. The room reflects how the agent is thinking, not what I told it to think about.

## How Dreamer Gets Its Input

Dreamer doesn't browse the internet. The only door into the room is a snapshot from my research agent.

I run a separate agent that does actual research. It does the scraping, summarizing, and tracking of my preferred topics. Every six hours, the walk script snapshots its vault and drops it into room/inbox-from-researchd/. During drift-from-research walks, Dreamer picks one thing from the snapshot and riffs on it. Other modes, it's just background context.

It's a one-way door. Research flows in. Dreamer never talks back.

If you don't have a research agent, Dreamer still works, just shift the weight toward pure-tangent or continue-project. Or drop any text file into the inbox on a cron. Dreamer doesn't care where the input comes from. It just needs something to bounce off of.

## What I've Learned So Far

**It takes time to warm up.** The first ten walks are short and generic. Dreamer doesn't know what it cares about yet. By walking fifty, the signal filter starts lighting up. By a hundred, it has genuine preferences.

**The SOUL matters more than the code.** I rewrote the walk script twice. I rewrote the SOUL five times. The SOUL changes everything about the quality of the walks. Get the personality right first, optimize the plumbing later.

**Echo detection is non-negotiable.** Without it, small models fixate. They find a topic they like and repeat it forever, inflating scores with repetition. The self-review kills that pattern. Add it early.

**Tend-the-room walks are underrated.** I almost cut them because they seemed like filler. They're not. That's where Dreamer prunes stale fascinations, notices abandoned projects, and resets. It's the mode where the agent learns about itself.

**Start slow.** Walks every hour, not every 30 minutes, until you see the patterns. Let it build its own memory and direction before you speed it up. The signal filter needs data to work. Rushing it fills the room with noise.

## Why It Works

Three constraints hold the whole thing together:

**Thinking and building are separated.** Walks are cheap, high-temperature, no-tools. Builds are expensive, low-temperature, and structured. An idea has to survive multiple walks over multiple days before it earns a build. This prevents the "build everything" failure mode.

**Signals decay.** A project that was exciting two weeks ago but hasn't come up since? Gone. The board reflects current interest, not historical interest. Dreamer has to keep caring.

**One sprint at a time.** The lock prevents context-switching. Finish one thing before starting the next. Dreamer keeps thinking during a sprint, but builds serialization.
