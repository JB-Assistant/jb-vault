## Summary

A solo operator can build a high-revenue AI-enabled services business by selling outcomes and automating delivery with AI. The post argues that the cost of AI infrastructure has collapsed, enabling software-like margins and near-zero risk for solo operators. The author labels this approach as "service-as-a-software" and emphasizes the leverage of being able to scale through automation rather than headcount.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. One person can generate revenue previously requiring a 30-person agency by leveraging AI delivery stacks.
2. The Service-as-a-Software model sells outcomes (more leads, more meetings, more revenue) and uses AI to automate ~90% of delivery.
3. Margins can approach 80% because AI reduces delivery costs and headcount.
4. Risk is low: no inventory, no warehouse, no large teams required; a laptop and outbound sales suffices.
5. There is a brief window where solo operators can outperform incumbents who lag in AI adoption; incumbents will catch up as AI becomes standard.
