## Full Article
Every small business owner has the same problem right now.
They've tried ChatGPT. They've seen the demos on X. They've downloaded three apps they never opened. But when they sit down at their desk Monday morning, nothing has changed. The inbox is still a mess. The calendar is still packed. The same manual processes eat the same hours every week.
What they DON'T need is another tool recommendation. 
They need someone to sit down, look at their actual workflows, and tell them exactly where to start.
That's the business. And five months into running this model, it's making me about $1,000/hour. 
Here's the full playbook so you can build the same thing.
PS: If you want the exact template I use to deliver the audit report you can grab it (free) at audittemplate.ai.

Why This Business Exists Right Now
There's a translation gap in the market that's getting wider, not smaller.
On one side: AI tools that are genuinely powerful. Claude can analyze a full meeting transcript and pull out action items in seconds. Fathom records and summarizes every call automatically. Zapier connects 6,000+ apps without code. These tools work.
On the other side: business owners who know they should be using AI but have no idea which of the 500 tools they've heard about actually solves their specific problem.
The people who learn to bridge that gap are going to stay busy. The tools will eventually get simpler. Everyone will eventually figure it out. But right now, the window is wide open and the demand is real.

The Offer: What You're Actually Selling
I charge $999 for what I call an AI Tools Assessment.
Here's what the client gets:
- A 45-minute recorded discovery interview
- A custom report with 3-7 specific tool recommendations
- A 30-minute walkthrough call to review the findings
- A 4-day quick-start implementation plan
The guarantee: 5+ hours returned per week, or full refund.
That sounds aggressive until you realize how much time most business owners waste on tasks AI solved two years ago. The average client I've worked with sees 6+ hours per week freed up, using tools that cost about $40/month combined.
That's a 25x monthly return on a one-time $999 investment. It practically sells itself once you frame it that way.

The Four-Phase Framework
Phase 1: The Discovery Call (45 Minutes)
This is where the entire assessment lives or dies. You're not pitching solutions. You're pulling problems.
The questions that surface the best opportunities:
1. Walk me through yesterday. Where did your time actually go?
2. What tasks do you dread but can't hand off to anyone?
3. Where does work pile up waiting on you specifically?
4. What have you tried to automate but couldn't figure out?
5. What's the one thing that, if it ran itself, would change your week?
The goal is hearing their pain in their own words. Not what they think they should automate. What's actually eating their time.
Record everything. I use Fathom for automatic transcription. That transcript is the raw material for everything that comes next.
Pro tip: Most people underestimate how much time they lose to context-switching. When they say 'I check email throughout the day,' that's usually 45-60 minutes of lost productivity they've never quantified. Ask follow-ups that surface the hidden time drains.

Phase 2: The AI-Powered Analysis
After the call, take the full transcript and feed it into Claude with this prompt:
Analyze this business owner interview transcript. Identify 5-7 specific areas where existing AI tools could save significant time each week.
For each opportunity:
- Name the specific tool(s) you'd recommend
- Estimate weekly hours saved
- Rate implementation difficulty (easy/medium/hard)
- Describe what the workflow looks like before and after
Focus on tools that are production-ready today, not experimental. Prioritize opportunities with the highest time savings and lowest implementation friction.
Claude catches patterns you'll miss. It connects dots across different parts of the conversation, things the client mentioned in passing that actually represent their biggest time sinks.
Review the output. Add recommendations from your own experience. Cut anything that feels like a stretch.

Phase 3: The Report
Build the final deliverable in Gamma (or your preferred presentation tool). Clean. Professional. Looks like something a $10,000 consulting firm would produce.
Report structure:
1. Executive summary (top 3 opportunities, total estimated hours saved)
2. Priority matrix (high impact + low effort = start here)
3. Tool recommendations with specific implementation steps
4. 4-day quick-start plan (what to set up on Day 1, Day 2, etc.)
5. Financial impact breakdown (hours saved x hourly rate = annual value)
6. Recommended next steps
This takes about 30 minutes to assemble from a template once you've done it a few times. Clients consistently think it took days.
Key insight: The priority matrix is the most valuable page in the report.

Phase 4: The Review Call (30 Minutes)
Screen-share the report. Walk through each recommendation. Answer questions. Let them absorb it.
Then ask three questions:
1. Which of these feels most urgent to you?
2. Do you want to implement these yourself, or would you like help?
3. What's your realistic timeline for getting started?
About 60% of my assessment clients want help with implementation. That's where the real revenue lives.

The Expansion Menu (Where the Real Money Is)
The $999 assessment is the door. Here's what's behind it:
- Process Redesign ($3,000-5,000)
- Automation Builds ($1,000-3,000)
- Knowledge Systems ($3,000+)
- Custom Skill Libraries ($3,000-5,000)
- Full Implementation ($5,000-10,000 + monthly retainer)

How To Get Started
1. Know the tool landscape (30 days hands-on with core stack: Claude, ChatGPT, Fathom, Gamma, Zapier, Make.com)
2. Practice the process (3-5 free assessments)
3. Start charging and raise your price ($199 -> $499 -> $999)
4. Build the backend (upsell menu, process templates, automation packages)

The Numbers That Matter
4 assessments/month = $3,996/month from assessments alone
60% conversion to implementation = 2-3 implementation projects
Average implementation deal: $3,000-5,000
Monthly revenue potential: $10,000-15,000 working 15-20 hours/week

Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't recommend tools you haven't tested
- Don't overwhelm them with options (3-5 recs is sweet spot)
- Don't skip the discovery call
- Don't undercharge because you feel like an imposter

Key Takeaways
- The AI consulting opportunity is translating technology for people who don't have time to figure it out themselves
- A $999 assessment typically returns 6+ hours/week for the client at $40/month in tool costs
- The 4-phase framework: discovery call -> AI analysis -> polished report -> review call
- 60% of assessment clients want implementation help at $3,000-10,000+
- All you need: tool fluency and the ability to ask good questions

The translation gap between 'AI can do this' and 'here's how to set it up for your business' is wide open. It won't last forever. The window is now.

## Summary
Corey Ganim outlines a high-margin business model centered on "AI Tools Assessments" for small businesses. He argues that while powerful AI tools like Claude, ChatGPT, and Zapier exist, most business owners are paralyzed by choice and lack the time to integrate them into their specific workflows. By charging $999 for a structured audit, consultants can bridge this "translation gap," identifying time-wasters and providing a clear implementation roadmap that guarantees significant weekly time savings.

The model follows a four-phase framework: Discovery, AI-powered analysis of the transcript, generation of a polished report using tools like Gamma, and a final review call. The assessment acts as a low-friction entry point for high-ticket implementation services which can range from $3,000 to $10,000+. Ganim emphasizes that the value lies not in proposing experimental technology, but in applying production-ready tools to solve real manual pain points identified through rigorous interviewing.

Overall, the strategy capitalizes on the current market window where AI capability is high but adoption knowledge is low. By providing a 25x monthly ROI on the client's investment, the service "practically sells itself," allowing consultants to generate substantial revenue with relatively low weekly hours.

## Key Takeaways
1. **The Translation Gap:** There is high demand for individuals who can translate generic AI capabilities into specific business workflow solutions.
2. **Standardized Offer:** A $999 "AI Tools Assessment" includes a discovery call, a custom report with 3-7 recommendations, a walkthrough, and a 4-day quick-start plan.
3. **Structured Discovery:** Identifying "tasks you dread" and "where work piles up" through recorded interviews is the foundation of a successful audit.
4. **AI-Assisted Analysis:** Consultants should use LLMs (like Claude) to analyze transcripts and identify specific automation opportunities and tool recommendations.
5. **High Upsell Potential:** 60% of audit clients convert to full implementation projects, which are significantly more lucrative ($3k - $10k+).
6. **Focus on Production-Ready Tools:** Avoid experimental tech; stick to reliable stacks like Claude, Zapier, Make.com, and Fathom.
7. **Value Framing:** Frame the cost against the hours saved (e.g., 5+ hours/week) to demonstrate an overwhelming ROI.
