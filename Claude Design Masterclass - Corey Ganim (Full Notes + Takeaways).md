## What Claude Design Actually Is

NOT Canva. NOT Figma. NOT a template generator.

It's a **collaborative design partner** in your browser:

- **Reads your brand** — imports codebase, design files, crawls your website. Auto-applies colors, fonts, style.
- **Understands context** — upload reference images, docs, use web capture tool
- **Exports anywhere** — PDF, PPTX, HTML, Canva, or internal link
- **Hands off to code** — packages into "handoff bundle" for Claude Code with one instruction

**Real use cases:**
- Landing pages (15 min)
- Pitch decks (20 min)
- Product prototypes (30-45 min)
- Social media graphics (batch of 5 in 10 min)
- One-pagers, sales sheets, case studies
- Interactive prototypes (voice, video, shaders, 3D)

## The Workflow

**Two-panel interface:** Left = chat with Claude. Right = design canvas.

### Step 1: Create Project → Auto Brand Integration
Your org's design system loads automatically. No uploading. No configuring. Your brand is already there.

### Step 2: Give Context
- Reference images ("Here's what competitors are doing")
- Design docs (brand guidelines PDFs)
- Content (copy, headlines, CTAs)
- Audience ("30-year-old SaaS founders")
- Constraints ("Minimal and clean, not playful")

**Rule:** The more specific, the fewer revisions.

### Step 3: Write Your Prompt
**You DON'T need design language.**

❌ Bad: "Use Helvetica sans-serif with 16px letter spacing on #2D3748 background"
✅ Good: "Create a landing page for a productivity tool. Audience is busy executives who value simplicity. Make it clean, professional, minimalist. Hero section with headline, subheadline, CTA button. Below that, three feature cards with icons."

**Prompt formula:**
1. **Goal:** What are you building?
2. **Layout:** How should it be organized?
3. **Content:** What information goes in it?
4. **Audience:** Who's this for?
5. **Tone:** What feeling should it convey?

**Example prompt (from article):**
> "Create a landing page for Return My Time, an AI automation agency for solopreneurs. The audience is busy business owners who are tired of manual work. Hero section with headline 'Reclaim 10 hours per week' and subheadline 'AI workflows built for non-technical entrepreneurs.' Include three feature sections highlighting: 1) Done-for-you setup, 2) Results in 7 days, 3) ROI-focused strategy. Make it feel premium but approachable, not corporate, not playful. Use our brand colors (deep blue and white). Add a CTA at the bottom: 'Start Your AI Audit'."

## How to Iterate (The Real Magic)

### Chat-Based Iterations (Big Changes)
- "Make this darker and more premium. Add more white space."
- "This feels too busy. Simplify it — remove testimonials, focus on three main features."
- "Can you add a dark mode toggle in the top right?"
- "Make the CTA button bigger and change it to orange."

### Inline Comments (Targeted Tweaks)
Click directly on element → request change. Faster than chat:
- Click button → "Make this taller"
- Click heading → "Reduce font size by 2px"
- Click section → "Add more padding on the left"

### Direct Editing (You Control Details)
Edit text directly on canvas. Claude maintains design consistency.

### Adjustment Knobs (Custom Sliders)
Claude creates sliders for:
- Spacing adjuster (tight → loose)
- Color intensity (subtle → bold)
- Typography weight (light → heavy)
- Component size (compact → spacious)

## Limitations (Research Preview)

1. **Complex animations** — Sophisticated transitions/scroll effects not supported yet
2. **3D rendering** — Basic 3D shapes work, intricate models don't
3. **Live data integration** — Static designs only, no dynamic dashboards
4. **Pixel-perfect precision** — About 95% there. Figma still wins for absolute control
5. **Context limits** — Very complex multi-section designs may need splitting
6. **Collaborative editing** — Turn-based, not Google Docs live collaboration

**Verdict:** These limitations don't matter for 90% of what solopreneurs need.
