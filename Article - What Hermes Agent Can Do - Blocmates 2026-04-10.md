**What is Hermes Agent?**

Hermes Agent is an agent harness built by @NousResearch, the team behind YaRN, Nomos, and Psyche. AI in its current state is static and stateless — you have a conversation, get results, close the tab, and everything is forgotten. Hermes is designed to be a persistent, self-hosted AI agent that:

- Lives on your machine or server
- Remembers everything across sessions
- Gets smarter the more you use it
- Can be reached from your phone via Telegram or Discord, even when you're not at your computer
- Offers a closed learning loop that turns every completed task into a reusable skill
- Turns every past conversation into searchable long-term memory
- Runs on any model you choose
- Executes code and terminal commands across six different backends
- Ships with a growing library of skills (animated video production, autonomous novel writing, and more)

When most people say AI, your brain synonymizes it with ChatGPT. Hermes is more like hiring a member of staff who never sleeps, remembers everything, gets better with more work, and is always available through any device.

Now, with the most recent updates, we may finally have a single agent interface that has completely fixed the productivity stack using AI.

**Layer 1: The Knowledge Layer**

The first layer doesn't begin with Nous Research — it begins with Andrej Karpathy, who proposed the "LLM-Wiki" concept: using LLMs to build personal knowledge bases for research topics, where a large fraction of token throughput goes into manipulating knowledge rather than just code.

The pain point: most LLMs today operate like RAGs — you drop files into ChatGPT, it retrieves the relevant chunk and gives you an answer. But ask a question requiring synthesis across multiple documents and it has to rediscover relevant fragments. There's no accumulation of knowledge, just rediscovery.

The LLM-Wiki solution: a system that operates more like a dynamic encyclopedia where all knowledge is structured, interlinked, persistent, and constantly accumulating with every new interaction, piece of data, or file added. Think of it like your brain as a library — every time you learn something new, a librarian files it somewhere. Over the years that librarian gets very good at knowing where everything is, how different ideas connect, and what's relevant when you need it. Your knowledge compounds.

Nous Research has implemented the LLM-Wiki as a "skill" in the Hermes Agent terminal. This forms the base layer — all raw notes, interactions, files, and information are permanently available, no context renewal needed. The agent permanently knows your domain and only gets more powerful.
