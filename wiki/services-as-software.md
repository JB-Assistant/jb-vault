# Services-as-Software

## Definition
Services-as-software is an operating model where the customer-facing contract still looks like services, but the delivery engine underneath runs like software: documented workflows, reusable systems, internal tools, AI agents, decision logs, and automation that reduce dependence on linear headcount.

## Key Ideas
- Human value concentrates in judgment, relationship, taste, and accountability.
- Repeatable delivery should be documented, systematized, and automated where possible.
- The agency needs workflows, SOPs, decision logs, escalation protocols, and tool-backed execution so no single person is structurally required.
- Revenue layers that compound with content or software-like distribution help offset churn and services cyclicality.
